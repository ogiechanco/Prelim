First Recognition

Published on February 21, 2025

During my senior high school years, I hardly gained any knowledge—perhaps due to the pandemic or my own lack of seriousness in studying. However, after graduating , reality hit me hard. I saw my friends, who were in the same academic strand as mine but from different schools, excelling in their studies and getting into universities with a solid knowledge base.

This realization overwhelmed me as I prepared for college, making me anxious about facing university life without the necessary foundation in my chosen field. That’s when I decided to start advanced studying. I didn’t want to be left behind, so before entering college, I took the initiative to learn basic programming, including Java, HTML, and CSS.

Looking back, it was one of the best decisions I made. When I started college, I found my programming subjects much easier to understand because of my prior self-study. My classmates even started calling me a "smurf," not knowing the effort and dedication I had put into learning those skills.

Achieving this milestone made me proud, as it ultimately led me to become a dean’s lister in my first year of college. I hope to maintain this achievement in the coming years. I’m sharing this story not to brag but to inspire other students—hard work and determination truly pay off!
